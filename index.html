About Me

Who I Am

Professional Life

I'm a jack of all trades who's worn many hats—from policy analyst to software engineer to technical consultant. I spent six years at Google working across payments, partnerships, and advertising policy, where I trained hundreds of people and launched global initiatives. Then I did something unexpected: I enrolled in a coding bootcamp and pivoted into software engineering.

That pivot led me through roles at fintech and e-commerce startups, where I built everything from fraud detection tools to full-stack applications—often as the sole engineer. These days, I'm channeling that same energy into a new direction: the travel and hospitality industry. I'm based in Bangkok, attending industry conferences, improving my Thai, and writing about the places I explore.

Beyond the Code

I've visited 45 countries and counting. For a while, I lived as a digital nomad, moving to a new city every few months—Bali, Lisbon, Buenos Aires were some favorites. I travel for discovery: the thrill of getting lost in a new place, the conversations with strangers, the meals you'd never find in a guidebook.

My style is adventure with a sprinkle of luxury. I'll hike through leeches in a Thai national park, then reward myself with a proper massage and good coffee. Moving from the US to Bangkok was one of the best decisions I've made—it put me closer to the places and experiences I love most.
